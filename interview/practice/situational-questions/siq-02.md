**2.** What was your role in the team? How many people? How did you divide work?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

I was a backend developer in a cross-functional team. Our team had around 8-10 people: 3-4 backend developers, 2-3 frontend developers, 1 QA, 1 PM, and a tech lead. We followed Agile with 2-week sprints.

Work was divided by domain ownership. I owned the search aggregation service and its satellite services. Another backend developer owned the inventory service, another handled the analytics pipeline. We used Jira for task tracking — the PM and tech lead groomed the backlog, and developers picked tasks during sprint planning based on their domain. For cross-domain features (like the natural language search), we'd form a small squad of 2-3 people and work together for 1-2 sprints.

The key thing that worked: domain ownership meant I deeply understood my services, could make fast decisions, and was on-call for them. The trade-off was knowledge silos — we mitigated this with code reviews across domains and a weekly knowledge-sharing session.
