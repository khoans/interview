**10.** How do you handle context switching between multiple tasks or projects?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

I minimize it as much as possible — context switching is a productivity killer. My approach: block time in my calendar for deep work (usually mornings), handle PR reviews and Slack after lunch, and batch small tasks together. I keep a running note in Notion with "where I left off" for each task so I can resume quickly.

In our project, context switching was inevitable — I maintained the search service while also building new features and sometimes fixing urgent production issues. When an adhoc task came in, I'd assess: is it truly urgent (production is broken) or can it wait until my current task is at a natural stopping point? If urgent, I'd commit my current work-in-progress to a branch, switch, fix, then come back.

The key: don't try to multitask — instead, serialize tasks with clean handoff points. The worst thing you can do is half-finish two things. I'd rather finish one task completely and be "late" starting the second than have two incomplete tasks by end of day.
