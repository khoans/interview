**240.** What is a security champion program? How do you embed security in engineering teams?

A security champion program designates one developer per team as the security point person -- not a full-time security engineer, but a developer who receives extra security training and advocates for secure practices within their team.

On our marketplace team, I acted as an informal security champion: reviewing Spring Security configurations in PRs, ensuring OAuth2 scopes were properly validated, flagging when someone tried to store sensitive data unencrypted, and being the go-to person for security questions. I kept up with OWASP Top 10 updates and shared relevant findings in team standups. When SonarCloud flagged issues, I helped teammates understand why the finding mattered and how to fix it properly, not just suppress the warning.

The trade-off is time investment -- being a security champion takes roughly 10-15% of your development time. But the alternative (waiting for a separate security team review) creates bottlenecks and means security feedback comes too late. Embedding security knowledge in the team scales much better than centralizing it.
