**315.** Kafka vs SQS vs RabbitMQ: When should you use each? What are the ordering, throughput, and operational trade-offs?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Use SQS for simple, serverless, managed queuing with minimal ops. Use Kafka for high-throughput event streaming with replay capability and ordering guarantees. Use RabbitMQ for complex routing patterns with flexible exchange types.

On our marketplace, we chose SQS because it was fully managed on AWS (zero operational overhead), integrated natively with our ECS services and Lambda functions, and our throughput was moderate (thousands of messages/minute, not millions). SQS FIFO queues gave us ordering per message group ID when needed. We used SNS+SQS fan-out for the pattern where one listing update went to multiple consumers. Kafka would have been overkill — we did not need event replay, log compaction, or millions of messages/second, and managing Kafka clusters would have added significant ops burden.

The trade-off: SQS is simple and serverless but messages disappear after consumption (no replay), and throughput caps at ~3,000 messages/second per queue. Kafka retains events for replay and handles millions/second but requires cluster management (or pay premium for MSK). RabbitMQ offers flexible routing but is the hardest to scale and operate. Pick based on your throughput needs and operational maturity.
