**303.** What is a modular monolith? When is it a better choice than jumping straight to microservices?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

A modular monolith is a single deployable application with strict internal module boundaries — each module has its own domain, database schema (or schema namespace), and communicates with other modules through well-defined interfaces, not direct database joins. It gives you the organizational benefits of microservices without the distributed systems complexity.

It is a better choice when: your team is small (under 15 devs), your domain boundaries are still evolving, or you do not need independent scaling per module. On our marketplace, our dealer management module could have been a modular monolith component rather than a separate service — it had low traffic, deployed on the same cadence as the user service, and rarely needed independent scaling. Extracting it as a microservice added network latency and operational overhead for minimal benefit.

The trade-off: a modular monolith is easier to refactor into microservices later because the boundaries are already clean. But it requires discipline — without strict code reviews, developers will take shortcuts and create cross-module dependencies that defeat the purpose. It is an organizational discipline problem, not a technical one.
