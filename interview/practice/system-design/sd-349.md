**349.** Circuit breaker vs retry with backoff: When do you cut off a failing dependency vs keep trying? Can you use both?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Yes, use both together. Retry with exponential backoff for transient failures (network blips, brief timeouts). Circuit breaker to stop retrying entirely when a dependency is consistently down, preventing cascading failures.

On our marketplace, when the search service called the dealer service for enrichment data, we configured Resilience4j with both patterns. A single timeout triggered a retry with exponential backoff (100ms, 200ms, 400ms, max 3 retries). But if 50% of requests failed within a 30-second window, the circuit breaker opened — all subsequent calls returned a fallback response (cached dealer data from Redis) instead of hammering the failing service. After 60 seconds, the circuit half-opened, allowing one test request through. If it succeeded, the circuit closed and normal traffic resumed.

The trade-off: retry without a circuit breaker amplifies the problem — if a service is down, retries multiply the load 3x, making recovery harder. Circuit breaker without retries gives up too quickly on transient issues. Together, retries handle the small stuff and the circuit breaker protects against sustained failures. The key tuning decision is the failure threshold — too sensitive and it opens on normal latency spikes; too lenient and it fails to protect.
