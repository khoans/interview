**180.** What are Kubernetes labels and selectors? How do they enable service discovery?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Labels are key-value pairs attached to Kubernetes objects (pods, services, nodes) for identification and grouping. Selectors filter objects by their labels. Services use label selectors to discover which pods should receive traffic -- a Service with selector app=listing-service routes to all pods with that label, regardless of their IP or node. This decouples service routing from pod lifecycle.

In our marketplace, this maps directly to how ECS service discovery worked. Our ALB target groups used tags (equivalent to labels) to route traffic. If a listing-service pod had labels app=listing-service, version=v2, env=production, the production Service selector (app=listing-service, env=production) would match it. During canary deployments, we could route to specific versions by adding a version selector. We also used labels for operational queries -- kubectl get pods -l team=search,tier=backend quickly showed all pods owned by the search team.

The trade-off: labels are free-form, so without conventions they become inconsistent. We enforced a labeling standard: app (service name), version, env, team, and tier were required on every pod. Missing labels meant missing from monitoring dashboards and service routing. Enforce labeling standards early -- retrofitting labels across hundreds of resources is painful.
