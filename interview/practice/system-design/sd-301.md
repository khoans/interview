**301.** Monolith vs Microservices: What are the signals that tell you it's time to break a monolith? What are the signals that microservices are overkill?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Break when: teams step on each other during deployments, one component's scaling needs differ drastically from others, or a single module's failure takes down the whole system. Microservices are overkill when: you have a small team (under 5-8 devs), the domain is not well understood yet, or deployment frequency is low.

Our automotive marketplace was microservices from the start because we had separate teams owning search (Solr-backed, CPU-intensive, scaled to 12 instances), listings ingestion (I/O-heavy, different deployment cadence), and user-facing APIs. Each had different scaling profiles and release cycles. But within each service, we kept things as a single deployable — we did not split the search service further into indexing and querying microservices because one team owned both and they shared the same Solr cluster. That would have been over-decomposition.

The trade-off: microservices buy you independent scaling and deployment but cost you distributed system complexity — network failures, data consistency challenges, and operational overhead. If your team is small and your domain boundaries are unclear, start monolithic and extract services only when the pain of coupling exceeds the pain of distribution.
