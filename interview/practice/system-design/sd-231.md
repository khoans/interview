**231.** What is a software bill of materials (SBOM)? Why is it important for supply chain security?

An SBOM is a comprehensive inventory of every component, library, and dependency in your software -- including versions, licenses, and sources. Think of it as a nutrition label for software.

On our automotive marketplace with dozens of Spring Boot microservices and a Next.js frontend, an SBOM would catalog every Maven and npm dependency (direct and transitive). When Log4Shell hit, teams with SBOMs could instantly query which services use Log4j instead of manually checking each repository. Tools like Syft or CycloneDX generate SBOMs in standard formats (SPDX, CycloneDX JSON). We relied on mvn dependency:tree and Dependabot, but a formal SBOM would have given us a single searchable inventory.

The lesson is that SBOMs are becoming mandatory (US Executive Order 14028 requires them for government software). The trade-off is generation and maintenance overhead -- SBOMs must be regenerated on every build to stay accurate, which means integrating the tooling into CI/CD.
