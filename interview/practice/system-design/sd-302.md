**302.** When should a startup use a monolith vs microservices? At what team size / user scale does the switch make sense?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Startups should almost always start with a monolith. The switch to microservices makes sense around 20-30 engineers or when distinct teams need independent deployment cycles, not at a specific user count.

Our marketplace started with microservices because it was a large enterprise project from day one — multiple teams, well-defined domains (search, listings, dealer management, user accounts), and a clear scaling target of 30M monthly users. But I have seen smaller projects where a monolith with good module boundaries would have been far more productive. The key signal is organizational: when team A cannot deploy without coordinating with team B and this happens weekly, that is the pain threshold.

The trade-off: premature microservices at a startup means you spend 60% of engineering time on infrastructure (service discovery, distributed tracing, CI/CD pipelines per service) instead of product features. A well-structured monolith with clean module boundaries can handle millions of users — the bottleneck is usually the database, not the application architecture.
