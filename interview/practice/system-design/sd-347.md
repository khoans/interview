**347.** Event sourcing vs traditional CRUD: When should you store events vs just the current state? What's the operational complexity?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Use event sourcing when you need a complete audit trail, the ability to replay and rebuild state, or when the history of changes is as important as the current state (financial systems, compliance, undo/redo). Use traditional CRUD for everything else.

On our marketplace, we used traditional CRUD for listings — dealers cared about the current listing state, not the history of every price change. However, for dealer activity auditing and compliance, we logged state changes as events to a separate audit table in PostgreSQL. This was not full event sourcing (we did not rebuild state from events) but gave us the audit trail regulators needed without the complexity. Full event sourcing would have been overkill — we did not need to "replay" a listing's history to derive its current state.

The trade-off: event sourcing gives you complete history, temporal queries ("what was the state at 3pm?"), and replay capability, but the operational complexity is significant — event schema evolution, event store growth (you never delete), projection rebuilding, and eventual consistency between the event store and read models. For most business applications, CRUD with an audit log gives you 80% of the benefit at 20% of the complexity.
