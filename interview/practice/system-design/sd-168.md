**168.** What is a PersistentVolume (PV) and PersistentVolumeClaim (PVC)? How does storage work in K8s?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

A PersistentVolume (PV) is a piece of storage provisioned in the cluster (EBS volume, NFS share, etc.). A PersistentVolumeClaim (PVC) is a request for storage by a pod -- it specifies size and access mode, and Kubernetes binds it to a matching PV. This decouples storage provisioning (admin concern) from storage consumption (developer concern). StorageClasses enable dynamic provisioning -- creating PVs on demand when a PVC is submitted.

In our marketplace, we kept stateful services on managed AWS offerings (RDS for PostgreSQL, ElastiCache for Redis, S3 for images) specifically to avoid managing persistent storage in containers. But for our EKS evaluation, we configured the AWS EBS CSI driver as a StorageClass, so when a StatefulSet pod requested a PVC of 100GB gp3, Kubernetes automatically provisioned an EBS volume and attached it. The volume followed the pod across restarts but was AZ-bound.

The trade-off: PVs tied to EBS are availability-zone-specific -- if a pod reschedules to a different AZ, the volume cannot follow. For cross-AZ workloads, you need EFS (NFS-based) or application-level replication. This is why managed databases (RDS Multi-AZ) are usually better than running databases in Kubernetes for production workloads.
