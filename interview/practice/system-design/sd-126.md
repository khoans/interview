**126.** What is SSL termination? Where should you terminate SSL in your architecture?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========
SSL/TLS termination is decrypting HTTPS traffic at a specific point in your architecture (typically the load balancer or reverse proxy) and forwarding plain HTTP to backend services. This offloads the CPU-intensive TLS decryption from application servers and centralizes certificate management.

**Real example:** On our automotive marketplace, we terminated TLS at the ALB (Application Load Balancer). CloudFront terminated TLS at the edge for static assets, then re-encrypted to the ALB using a separate TLS connection. The ALB terminated TLS and forwarded plain HTTP to ECS targets on port 8080. This meant our Spring Boot services did not need to manage TLS certificates or perform encryption — the ALB handled it. Certificate management was centralized in ACM (one certificate per ALB, auto-renewed). Backend traffic between ALB and ECS was unencrypted but within the VPC, which AWS considers a trusted network boundary.

**Trade-off:** Terminating at the load balancer means internal traffic is unencrypted — a compromised VPC could see plaintext traffic. For PCI DSS or strict compliance, you may need end-to-end encryption (re-encryption from ALB to backend or mTLS). We accepted the ALB termination approach because our VPC was tightly locked down with Security Groups and NACLs, and the operational simplicity was worth the marginal risk.
