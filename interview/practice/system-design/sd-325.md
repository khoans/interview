**325.** When should you denormalize your database? What is the cost of denormalization vs the cost of JOINs?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Denormalize when read performance is critical and the data is read far more often than written. Keep normalized when data integrity matters more than read speed or when write patterns are frequent and varied.

On our marketplace, we denormalized listing search results. Instead of joining listings + dealers + locations + pricing tables on every search query, we built a denormalized Solr document containing all fields a user sees in search results. This turned a 5-table join into a single document read, dropping search latency from 200ms to under 50ms for 30M monthly users. The cost was maintaining consistency — every time a dealer updated their name, we had to update it in both PostgreSQL (source of truth) and Solr (denormalized view) via an async SQS event.

The trade-off: denormalization trades write complexity for read speed. You duplicate data, which means update anomalies are possible if your sync mechanism fails. JOINs keep data clean but become expensive as tables grow into millions of rows — especially multi-table joins with sorting and filtering. Denormalize your hot read paths; keep your write paths normalized.
