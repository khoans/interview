**156.** What is SAML? How does it differ from OAuth2/OIDC?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

SAML (Security Assertion Markup Language) is an XML-based standard for exchanging authentication and authorization data between an Identity Provider (IdP) and a Service Provider (SP). It is primarily used for enterprise SSO in browser-based flows. Unlike OAuth2/OIDC which uses JSON/JWT and is API-friendly, SAML uses XML assertions, is browser-redirect-heavy, and does not work well for mobile apps or SPAs.

In our marketplace, our enterprise dealer partners (large dealership groups with 50+ locations) used SAML SSO so their employees could access our dealer portal using their corporate Active Directory credentials. We integrated with their SAML IdP (Azure AD) -- the user authenticated at their corporate login page, and we received a SAML assertion with their roles and dealership IDs. For our consumer-facing app and API integrations, we used OAuth2/OIDC because it is lighter, mobile-friendly, and works well with REST APIs.

The lesson: SAML is ideal for enterprise SSO where the IdP is an established corporate directory. OAuth2/OIDC is better for modern apps, APIs, and mobile. We maintained both because our user base included enterprise dealers (SAML) and individual consumers (OIDC).
