**266.** What is passwordless authentication? Explain magic links, passkeys, and biometrics.

Passwordless authentication eliminates passwords entirely. **Magic links** send a one-time login URL via email -- clicking it authenticates the user. **Passkeys** (FIDO2/WebAuthn) use public-key cryptography tied to a device -- the private key never leaves the device, preventing phishing. **Biometrics** use fingerprint, face, or voice recognition as the authentication factor.

On our marketplace, we considered magic links for consumer accounts (casual car shoppers who visit infrequently and forget passwords). The flow: user enters email, receives a link with a time-limited signed token (JWT with 10-minute expiry), clicks it, and gets an authenticated session. For dealer accounts handling high-value operations, passkeys would be the ideal upgrade from TOTP-based MFA since they are phishing-resistant.

The trade-off: magic links depend on email security (if someone has access to your email, they have access to your account) and add login friction (switching to email app). Passkeys require device support and user education. Biometrics raise privacy concerns and cannot be revoked if compromised (you cannot change your fingerprint). Each method trades one risk for another.
