**209.** What is AWS Shield? Explain Standard vs Advanced for DDoS protection.

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

AWS Shield protects against DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) attacks. Shield Standard is free and automatically protects all AWS resources against common Layer 3/4 attacks (SYN floods, UDP reflection, DNS amplification). Shield Advanced is a paid service ($3,000/month) that adds Layer 7 protection, real-time attack visibility, DDoS Response Team (DRT) access, cost protection (credits for scaling costs during attacks), and integration with WAF for application-layer defense.

In our marketplace, we used Shield Standard (free, automatic) plus WAF for Layer 7 protection. Shield Standard handled the volumetric attacks -- it automatically detected and mitigated SYN floods targeting our ALB. For our 30M users/month platform, we evaluated Shield Advanced when we experienced a sustained HTTP flood that bypassed Shield Standard. The WAF rate-limiting rules mitigated it, but Shield Advanced would have provided the DRT to respond faster and automatic WAF rule deployment during attacks. We decided the $3K/month was not justified since WAF handled our threat level.

The trade-off: Shield Standard is sufficient for most applications -- it handles 99% of DDoS attacks transparently. Shield Advanced is for high-value targets (e-commerce, financial services) where a sustained DDoS could cause significant business loss. The cost protection alone (AWS credits for auto-scaling costs during attacks) can justify the price for large-scale applications.
