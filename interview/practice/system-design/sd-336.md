**336.** ECS vs Kubernetes: When is ECS "good enough" vs when do you need full Kubernetes?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

ECS is good enough when you are all-in on AWS, have straightforward container orchestration needs, and want minimal ops overhead. Kubernetes is needed when you require multi-cloud portability, complex scheduling, custom operators, or have a platform team that can manage it.

On our marketplace, ECS with Fargate was the right choice. We were 100% on AWS, had straightforward deployment needs (rolling updates, auto-scaling based on CPU/memory), and our team of 15-20 developers did not have a dedicated platform engineering team to manage a Kubernetes cluster. ECS Fargate gave us serverless containers — no EC2 instances to patch, no control plane to manage, and native integration with ALB, CloudWatch, and IAM. We went from Elastic Beanstalk to ECS in a few weeks with minimal learning curve.

The trade-off: Kubernetes gives you portability (same manifests work on AWS, GCP, Azure), a massive ecosystem (Helm charts, service mesh, custom operators), and fine-grained control. But it requires significant expertise and operational investment. If your answer to "Will we ever leave AWS?" is "No," ECS is simpler, cheaper to operate, and just as capable for 95% of workloads.
