**258.** What is network latency? Explain RTT (Round Trip Time) and how to measure it.

Network latency is the time it takes for data to travel from source to destination. RTT (Round Trip Time) is the total time for a packet to go from client to server and back -- it includes propagation delay (speed of light through fiber), processing delay (routers, servers), and queuing delay (waiting in buffers). Measured via ping, traceroute, or application-level metrics.

On our marketplace, we measured RTT at multiple levels: CloudFront edge to user (typically 5-20ms within the US), ALB to ECS service (sub-1ms within the same AZ), and ECS to RDS PostgreSQL (1-2ms within the same VPC). New Relic tracked end-to-end response times, and we set P95 latency alerts at 500ms for our search API. The biggest latency contributor was the Solr query itself (50-200ms), not network hops.

The lesson: geographic distance is the latency you cannot optimize away -- speed of light means roughly 1ms per 200km of fiber. This is why our multi-region deployment (us-east-1 for east coast users, us-west-2 for west coast) and CloudFront edge caching were essential.
