**337.** Managed services (RDS, ElastiCache) vs self-hosted: When is the premium worth it? When should you self-host?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Managed services are worth it when your team is small, uptime is critical, and the operational overhead of self-hosting exceeds the cost premium. Self-host when you need deep customization, cost savings at extreme scale, or run on-premise.

On our marketplace, we used managed everything: RDS for PostgreSQL, ElastiCache for Redis, and later ECS Fargate for containers. RDS handled automated backups, patching, failover, and read replicas with one click. The premium was roughly 30-40% over self-hosted EC2 PostgreSQL, but it eliminated the need for a DBA. Before that, our team had spent weeks dealing with a self-hosted PostgreSQL failover issue on Elastic Beanstalk — after switching to RDS Multi-AZ, failover was automatic and took under 60 seconds.

The trade-off: managed services limit your customization — you cannot tune kernel parameters, install custom extensions easily, or run bleeding-edge versions. At extreme scale (thousands of instances), the managed premium becomes significant. But for most teams, the cost of one database outage or one engineer spending a week on patching far exceeds the managed service premium. Self-host only when you have dedicated ops engineers and a specific technical requirement that managed services cannot fulfill.
