**218.** What is cross-account access in AWS? How do you securely share resources between accounts?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Cross-account access lets identities in one AWS account access resources in another. The two main approaches: (1) IAM role assumption -- create a role in Account B with a trust policy allowing Account A, then Account A calls sts:AssumeRole to get temporary credentials. (2) Resource-based policies -- attach a policy on the resource (S3 bucket, KMS key, SQS queue) granting access to the other account's principal directly.

In our marketplace, we had three accounts (dev, staging, production) and used both approaches: (1) Role assumption -- our GitHub Actions CI/CD in the dev account assumed a deployment role in the production account to update ECS services. The trust policy required the OIDC identity from GitHub Actions and an ExternalId for additional security. (2) Resource-based -- our production S3 bucket had a bucket policy allowing the staging account's ETL role to read listing data for integration testing. (3) KMS key policy granted cross-account Decrypt access to the staging account for reading encrypted snapshots copied from production.

The trade-off: role assumption is more secure (requires explicit AssumeRole call, temporary credentials, can enforce MFA) but adds complexity. Resource-based policies are simpler but harder to audit centrally -- permissions are scattered across resources. We used AWS IAM Access Analyzer to continuously monitor cross-account access and flag any unintended sharing. Always use ExternalId in trust policies to prevent the confused deputy problem.
