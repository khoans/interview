**281.** Your application is being targeted by a credential stuffing attack. What immediate steps do you take?

First, detect the pattern: credential stuffing shows as a spike in failed logins across MANY different accounts from many IPs (unlike brute force which targets one account). Check New Relic and Kibana dashboards for login failure rate anomalies.

On our marketplace, my immediate response would be: (1) Enable enhanced rate limiting on the login endpoint at CloudFront WAF -- drop requests exceeding 5 login attempts per minute per IP, (2) Activate CAPTCHA for all login attempts temporarily via feature flag, (3) Block the top offending IP ranges at WAF, (4) Force password resets for any accounts that had successful logins from suspicious IPs, (5) Notify affected users via email, (6) Enable MFA enforcement for high-value accounts (dealer admins). We would monitor the attack in real-time via Kibana, looking for the attacker adapting (rotating IPs, throttling requests to evade rate limits).

The lesson: you cannot fully prevent credential stuffing because the credentials are valid -- users reused passwords from other breaches. Long-term defenses include mandatory MFA, breached password checking at registration, and behavioral analysis (flagging logins from new devices/locations).
