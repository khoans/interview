**177.** What is a rolling update in Kubernetes? How do you configure maxSurge and maxUnavailable?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

A rolling update replaces old pods with new ones incrementally, ensuring zero downtime. maxSurge controls how many extra pods can exist above the desired count during the update (e.g., 25% of 4 = 1 extra pod). maxUnavailable controls how many pods can be unavailable during the update (e.g., 25% of 4 = 1 pod can be down). Together, they control the speed vs safety trade-off of deployments.

In our marketplace, we configured ECS rolling deployments similarly. For our listing service (4 instances), we used minimumHealthyPercent=75 and maximumPercent=150, equivalent to maxUnavailable=1 and maxSurge=2 in Kubernetes. This meant during deployment: 1 old instance could be stopped while 2 new instances were starting, ensuring at least 3 healthy instances served traffic at all times. For our search service, which had warm-up time for Solr caches, we used maxSurge=1 and maxUnavailable=0 -- never remove an old pod until the new one passes readiness checks.

The trade-off: maxSurge=0 with maxUnavailable=1 saves resources but reduces capacity during updates. maxSurge=100% with maxUnavailable=0 (blue-green style) guarantees zero capacity loss but doubles resource usage momentarily. Pick based on your service's sensitivity to capacity reduction.
