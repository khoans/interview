**360.** How did you handle a security incident or vulnerability discovery in your project? Walk me through the process from detection to resolution.

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

We followed a structured incident response process: detect, contain, investigate, fix, verify, and document.

On our marketplace, we discovered through ECR image scanning that a critical Log4j vulnerability (Log4Shell) existed in one of our service dependencies. **Detection**: Automated ECR scan flagged CVE-2021-44228 as CRITICAL, SNS notification hit our security Slack channel within minutes. **Containment**: We immediately added a WAF rule to block JNDI lookup strings in request headers and body — this mitigated active exploitation while we worked on the fix. **Investigation**: We used `mvn dependency:tree` to identify which transitive dependency pulled in the vulnerable Log4j version, assessed which services were affected (all Java services), and checked CloudFront and ALB logs in Kibana for any exploitation attempts. **Fix**: Updated Log4j to patched version across all services, merged PRs with expedited single-reviewer approval, rebuilt all Docker images, and deployed via rolling update on ECS within 4 hours of detection. **Verification**: Ran ECR scans on all new images to confirm clean, monitored logs for 24 hours for any residual exploitation patterns. **Documentation**: Filed an incident report documenting timeline, impact assessment (no evidence of exploitation), root cause, and preventive measures (added dependency scanning to CI, not just image scanning).

Lesson learned: the WAF mitigation bought us hours of breathing room. Having a pre-planned containment step — even imperfect — is more valuable than rushing a code fix under pressure.
