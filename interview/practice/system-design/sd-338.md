**338.** Multi-region vs single-region: When is multi-region deployment justified? What is the real cost (money, complexity, consistency)?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Multi-region is justified when you need disaster recovery with near-zero RTO, serve users in geographically distant locations with strict latency requirements, or when regulatory compliance mandates data residency.

On our marketplace, we deployed across us-east-1 and us-west-2. With 30M US users spread coast to coast, multi-region reduced latency for West Coast users by 30-40ms. More critically, it gave us disaster recovery — when us-east-1 had an AWS outage, Route 53 health checks automatically failed over to us-west-2 within minutes. The cost was significant: double the infrastructure spend, cross-region data replication for PostgreSQL (RDS cross-region read replicas), DynamoDB Global Tables for session data, and engineering effort to handle replication lag.

The trade-off: multi-region roughly doubles your infrastructure cost and significantly increases complexity — data consistency across regions is the hardest problem (which region is the source of truth during a split-brain?). For most applications, a single region with Multi-AZ deployment within that region provides sufficient availability. Only go multi-region when the business truly requires it — either for latency, compliance, or RTO under 5 minutes.
