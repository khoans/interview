**236.** What is vulnerability management? How do you prioritize CVEs (CVSS scoring)?

Vulnerability management is the continuous process of identifying, evaluating, prioritizing, and remediating security vulnerabilities. CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System) rates vulnerabilities from 0-10 based on exploitability, impact, and environmental factors.

On our marketplace, we used Dependabot and SonarCloud to identify CVEs. For prioritization: CVSS 9-10 (Critical) like Log4Shell got immediate patching within hours; CVSS 7-8.9 (High) were scheduled for the current sprint; CVSS 4-6.9 (Medium) went into the backlog. But CVSS alone is not enough -- context matters. A high-CVSS vulnerability in a library only used in an internal admin tool is less urgent than a medium-CVSS issue in our public-facing vehicle search API serving 30M users/month.

The lesson: prioritize by exploitability in YOUR context, not just the CVSS score. We also tracked mean-time-to-remediate (MTTR) as a team metric -- critical CVEs had to be patched within 48 hours, which sometimes meant dropping sprint work.
