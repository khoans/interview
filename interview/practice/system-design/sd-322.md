**322.** PostgreSQL vs MySQL: When would you choose one over the other? What are the feature differences that matter?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Choose PostgreSQL for complex queries, JSON support, full-text search, advanced data types, and strict SQL compliance. Choose MySQL for simpler workloads, high read throughput with replication, and when your team or hosting already standardizes on it.

On our marketplace, we chose PostgreSQL on RDS because we needed: JSONB columns for flexible vehicle attributes (different car types have different specs), advanced indexing (partial indexes, GIN indexes for array fields), CTEs for complex reporting queries, and robust concurrent write handling with MVCC. MySQL's simpler query optimizer would have struggled with our multi-table joins across listings, pricing, dealer, and location tables. PostgreSQL's query planner consistently produced better execution plans for our complex search aggregations.

The trade-off: PostgreSQL is more feature-rich but historically has slightly more memory overhead and a steeper learning curve for tuning. MySQL is simpler, has faster simple reads, and a larger ecosystem of managed hosting options. For most CRUD apps, either works fine — the difference matters when you need advanced SQL features, complex queries, or strict data integrity.
