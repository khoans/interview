**314.** Event-driven vs request-driven architecture: When should you switch from REST calls to events? What are the debugging challenges?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Switch to events when you have multiple consumers that need to react to the same action, when you need loose coupling between producer and consumer, or when the producer should not care who consumes the data.

On our marketplace, listing updates were event-driven — when a dealer modified a listing, the listings service published an event to SQS/SNS. Multiple consumers reacted independently: search service reindexed in Solr, analytics recorded the change, and the notification service alerted saved-search users. If we had used REST calls, the listings service would have needed to know about every consumer and call each synchronously — tight coupling and slow responses.

The debugging challenge is the biggest trade-off. With REST, you trace a request through one call chain. With events, a failure in the notification service does not show up in the listing service's logs — you need distributed tracing and correlation IDs across all consumers. We used New Relic with correlation IDs propagated through SQS message attributes, plus dead-letter queues to capture failed events. Without these tools, event-driven debugging is essentially impossible.
