**305.** Serverless vs containers: When should you use Lambda/Cloud Functions vs ECS/Kubernetes? What are the cost, latency, and operational trade-offs?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Use serverless for event-driven, bursty, short-lived workloads. Use containers for steady-state, latency-sensitive, or long-running services.

On our marketplace, we used both. Core services (search, listings API) ran on ECS with Fargate because they needed consistent sub-100ms latency, long-lived connections, and steady traffic from 30M monthly users — cold starts were unacceptable. But we used Lambda for event-driven tasks: image resizing when dealers uploaded vehicle photos, SQS message processing for listing updates, and scheduled cleanup jobs. Lambda was perfect there — bursty, stateless, and we paid only per invocation instead of running idle containers.

The trade-off: Lambda has cold start latency (1-3 seconds for Java, which is why we used Node.js for Lambdas), a 15-minute execution limit, and gets expensive at sustained high throughput. ECS costs more at low traffic but gives predictable latency and full control. The rule of thumb: if your service processes more than a few requests per second consistently, containers are cheaper and faster.
