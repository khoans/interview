**304.** Microservices vs SOA (Service-Oriented Architecture): What's the real difference and when does it matter?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

The real difference is scope and governance. SOA uses a centralized enterprise service bus (ESB) for communication and often shares databases across services. Microservices favor decentralized communication (direct HTTP/messaging), database-per-service, and team autonomy. SOA is top-down enterprise governance; microservices are bottom-up team ownership.

On our marketplace, we followed the microservices approach — each service owned its database (search had Solr, listings had PostgreSQL, session data in DynamoDB), communicated via REST and SQS, and each team deployed independently via GitHub Actions. If we had used SOA, we would have routed everything through a central ESB, shared a single database, and coordinated releases centrally. That would have been a bottleneck at our scale with multiple independent teams.

The trade-off: SOA provides better centralized governance and is easier for smaller organizations that want service reuse without full microservices complexity. Microservices give more team autonomy but require mature DevOps culture — CI/CD per service, distributed tracing, and service mesh. The distinction matters most when choosing communication and data ownership patterns.
