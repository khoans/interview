**239.** What is threat modeling? Explain STRIDE methodology.

Threat modeling is a structured process for identifying security threats to a system before they are exploited. STRIDE categorizes threats into six types: **Spoofing** (impersonating users), **Tampering** (modifying data), **Repudiation** (denying actions), **Information Disclosure** (data leaks), **Denial of Service** (making service unavailable), and **Elevation of Privilege** (gaining unauthorized access).

On our marketplace, applying STRIDE to the vehicle listing flow: Spoofing -- could someone post a listing as another dealer? (mitigated by OAuth2 tokens with dealer-scoped claims). Tampering -- could someone modify listing prices in transit? (mitigated by HTTPS via CloudFront). Information Disclosure -- could someone access other users saved searches? (mitigated by row-level security in PostgreSQL). Denial of Service -- could someone flood our Solr search API? (mitigated by rate limiting at ALB). Elevation of Privilege -- could a regular user access dealer admin endpoints? (mitigated by Spring Security role-based access).

The trade-off: threat modeling is time-consuming, so you focus it on high-value flows (authentication, payments, data access) rather than modeling every endpoint.
