**65.** What is DDoS protection? What strategies exist at different layers?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) overwhelms a system with traffic to make it unavailable. Protection strategies vary by layer: Layer 3/4 (network/transport): absorb volumetric attacks with massive bandwidth (AWS Shield, CloudFlare). Layer 7 (application): rate limiting, WAF rules, CAPTCHA challenges to filter malicious requests from legitimate ones.

In our project, we had multi-layer DDoS protection. AWS Shield Standard (free, automatic) protected against common Layer 3/4 volumetric attacks (SYN floods, UDP reflection) at the AWS network edge. CloudFront CDN absorbed traffic spikes — vehicle images served from 200+ edge locations meant attackers couldn't overwhelm our origin servers for static content. At Layer 7, AWS WAF rate-limited requests per IP (100/minute for search, 1000/minute for authenticated dealer APIs). During one suspected DDoS event, our search endpoint received 50x normal traffic from a botnet. CloudFront absorbed the static content requests, WAF rate-limited the API calls, and our ALB auto-scaled to handle the remaining legitimate traffic. The attack cost us ~$200 in extra CloudFront bandwidth but zero downtime.

The trade-off: advanced DDoS protection (AWS Shield Advanced: $3K/month) provides dedicated response teams and cost protection but is expensive for most applications. CloudFlare is a popular alternative for smaller budgets. The lesson: for most applications, CloudFront + WAF rate limiting + Shield Standard is sufficient. The key insight is that CDN is your best DDoS mitigation — it distributes traffic across the globe so no single origin is overwhelmed.
