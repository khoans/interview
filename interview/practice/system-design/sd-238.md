**238.** What is penetration testing vs vulnerability scanning? When do you do each?

Vulnerability scanning is automated, broad, and frequent -- tools like Dependabot or Trivy continuously scan for known CVEs in your dependencies and infrastructure. Penetration testing is manual, deep, and targeted -- a human tester actively tries to exploit your application like a real attacker would.

On our marketplace, vulnerability scanning ran continuously: Dependabot scanned dependencies daily, SonarCloud analyzed every PR, and ECR scanned images on push. Penetration testing happened quarterly or before major releases -- external testers attempted to bypass our Spring Security OAuth2 flows, exploit our search API (Apache Solr), or escalate privileges through our dealer management endpoints. Pen testers found issues automated tools missed, like business logic flaws where a dealer could modify another dealer listing by manipulating the listing ID.

The lesson: vulnerability scanning finds known problems at scale; penetration testing finds unknown problems in depth. You need both. The trade-off is cost -- pen tests are expensive ($10-50K per engagement) so you must scope them strategically.
