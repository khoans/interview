**324.** When should you use a database-per-service vs shared database in microservices? What are the data consistency trade-offs?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

Use database-per-service when services have different data models, need independent scaling, or when teams must deploy without coordinating schema changes. Use a shared database when services are tightly coupled, need cross-service transactions, or your team is small and the operational overhead of multiple databases is not justified.

On our marketplace, we used database-per-service: search owned Solr, listings and dealers owned PostgreSQL (separate schemas), sessions owned DynamoDB, and caching used ElastiCache Redis. Each team could evolve their schema independently via Flyway migrations without coordinating with other teams. The trade-off was data consistency — when a dealer updated their profile, the dealer service updated PostgreSQL but the search service had stale dealer info in Solr until the async SQS event was processed (2-5 second delay).

The trade-off: database-per-service gives you independence and resilience (one database going down does not affect all services) but sacrifices strong consistency — you accept eventual consistency and must handle it with events, sagas, or reconciliation jobs. A shared database gives you joins and transactions across services but creates deployment coupling — one bad migration can break every service.
