**15.** What are ACID properties? Why are they important for transactions?

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

ACID stands for Atomicity (all or nothing), Consistency (data stays valid), Isolation (concurrent transactions don't interfere), Durability (committed data survives crashes). Together they guarantee reliable transactions.

In our project, ACID mattered most for dealer operations. When a dealer purchased a "featured listing" package, the transaction had to: deduct the payment, update the listing's featured status, and create an audit record. If the payment succeeded but the listing update failed, we'd have charged the dealer for nothing. PostgreSQL's ACID guarantees ensured all three operations either committed together or rolled back entirely. We used Spring's @Transactional annotation with the default isolation level (READ_COMMITTED) for most operations. For the pricing service where concurrent dealer updates could cause lost updates, we used optimistic locking with a version column.

The trade-off: stricter isolation levels (SERIALIZABLE) prevent more anomalies but reduce throughput due to locking. READ_COMMITTED was sufficient for 95% of our use cases and kept throughput high. The lesson: don't default to the strictest level — understand your actual concurrency patterns and choose accordingly.
