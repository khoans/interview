**200.** How do you handle certificate management in Kubernetes? Explain cert-manager and Let's Encrypt integration.

========== THIS SECTION IS THE ANSWER ==========

cert-manager is a Kubernetes operator that automates the issuance and renewal of TLS certificates. It integrates with Let's Encrypt (free CA), AWS ACM, HashiCorp Vault, and other issuers. You define a Certificate resource specifying the domain and issuer, and cert-manager handles the ACME challenge, creates the certificate, stores it as a K8s Secret, and renews it before expiration.

In our marketplace, we used AWS ACM for TLS certificates on our ALB/CloudFront -- ACM handled auto-renewal transparently. Mapped to Kubernetes: install cert-manager, create a ClusterIssuer pointing to Let's Encrypt production ACME server with DNS01 or HTTP01 challenge solver, then annotate Ingress resources with cert-manager.io/cluster-issuer=letsencrypt-prod. cert-manager automatically provisions certificates, stores them in Secrets referenced by the Ingress, and renews 30 days before expiration. For internal mTLS certificates between services, Istio CA or cert-manager with a self-signed CA handles issuance and rotation.

The trade-off: Let's Encrypt certificates are free but expire every 90 days (forcing automated renewal) and only validate domain ownership, not organization identity. For customer-facing sites needing EV (Extended Validation) certificates, you still need a commercial CA. Also, cert-manager adds an operator to manage -- if it fails, certificate renewals stop and services get TLS errors. Monitor cert-manager health and certificate expiration dates with Prometheus alerts.
