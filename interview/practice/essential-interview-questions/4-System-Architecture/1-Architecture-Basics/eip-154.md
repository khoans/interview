## 154. What are the disadvantages of microservices?

**Microservices introduce distributed system complexity: network failures, eventual consistency, distributed tracing, operational overhead, and data integrity challenges.**

The downsides are technical and operational — not organizational.

**Key disadvantages:**
- **Network latency** — method calls become network requests (1ms → 50ms)
- **Distributed failures** — one service down cascades to others (circuit breakers required)
- **Eventual consistency** — no distributed transactions, data sync delays
- **Operational overhead** — 10 services need 10x monitoring, logging, deployment pipelines
- **Data integrity** — no ACID across services, complex sagas for multi-service operations

**Trade-off:**
- Team autonomy — deploy independently
- But: debugging requires distributed tracing across 5+ services
- Independent scaling — but network calls dominate latency budget

**Real-world example:**
Our order flow: cart → checkout → payment → inventory → shipping. In monolith: one transaction, 200ms. In microservices: 5 network calls, 800ms (4x slower). Payment succeeded, inventory update failed — order in limbo. Implemented saga pattern with compensating transactions — complexity increased 3x. Debugging required correlating logs across 5 services (distributed tracing). Operational overhead: 8 services, 3 on-call engineers, 20 alerts/night vs. 2 alerts/night with monolith.

**Rule of thumb:** Microservices are a last resort for scaling teams, not a first choice for performance. Only adopt when monolith deployment coordination is blocking releases weekly.
