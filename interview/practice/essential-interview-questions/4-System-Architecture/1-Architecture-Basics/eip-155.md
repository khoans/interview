## 155. When would you choose monolith over microservices?

**Choose monolith when you have a small team (<20 developers), simple domain, need fast iteration, or lack operational maturity for distributed systems.**

Monoliths are underrated — they're the right choice for 80% of applications.

**When monolith wins:**
- **Small team** — <20 developers, coordination is manageable
- **Simple domain** — no clear service boundaries yet
- **Fast iteration** — early-stage startup, pivoting weekly
- **Limited ops** — no dedicated DevOps, monitoring, or on-call rotation
- **Tight consistency** — ACID transactions across all data

**Trade-off:**
- Simpler deployment — one artifact, one server
- But: scaling requires replicating entire application
- Faster development initially — but technical debt accumulates

**Real-world example:**
Our startup had 10 developers, building MVP for product-market fit. Chose monolith — deployed 3 times/day, iterated fast. 18 months later: 100K users, profitable. Investors pushed for microservices. We analyzed: deployment took 15 minutes, zero coordination conflicts, team velocity high. Stayed monolithic. Two years later: 500K users, 25 developers. Deployment time: 45 minutes, merge conflicts weekly. Split into 4 services (user, catalog, orders, billing). Right timing — not too early, not too late.

**Rule of thumb:** Monolith until it hurts. Signs it's time: deployment frequency drops below weekly, team coordination meetings exceed 5 hours/week, or one component's scaling needs dominate infrastructure costs.
