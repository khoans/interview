## 151. What is monolithic architecture?

**Monolithic architecture is a single, unified application where all components run in one process — UI, business logic, data access tightly coupled.**

It's the default architecture for most applications. Simple to build, deploy, and scale — until complexity becomes unmanageable.

**Key characteristics:**
- Single codebase — one repository, one build artifact
- Centralized database — all services share the same schema
- Tight coupling — components call each other via method calls
- Deploy as one unit — change one feature, redeploy everything

**Trade-off:**
- Simple development — no distributed system complexity
- Fast deployment initially — one JAR, one server
- But: codebase grows unmanageable, deployments risky, scaling inefficient (replicate entire app)

**Real-world example:**
Our e-commerce platform started as a monolith — 50K lines, 3 developers, deployed daily. Two years later: 500K lines, 30 developers, deployments took 2 hours, one bug took down the entire site. Scaling required replicating everything — even though only checkout was slow. We could have stayed monolithic longer, but team productivity dropped 60% due to merge conflicts and coordination overhead.

**Rule of thumb:** Start monolithic for small teams and simple domains. Split when deployment frequency drops, team coordination costs exceed benefits, or specific components need independent scaling.
