## 152. What is microservices architecture?

**Microservices architecture breaks an application into small, independent services — each owns a business capability, deploys separately, communicates over network.**

It's about trading deployment complexity for team autonomy and independent scaling.

**Key characteristics:**
- Decentralized — each service has its own database, logic, deployment pipeline
- Business-aligned — services mirror organizational structure (Conway's Law)
- Independent scaling — scale only what's needed
- Failure isolation — one service down doesn't crash everything

**Trade-off:**
- Team autonomy — deploy without coordinating with 10 other teams
- But: distributed system complexity (network failures, eventual consistency, distributed tracing)
- Operational overhead — 10 services need 10x monitoring, logging, alerting

**Real-world example:**
Our monolith had 30 developers, merge conflicts daily, deployments took 2 hours. Split into 8 microservices: catalog, pricing, inventory, cart, checkout, payment, shipping, notifications. Each team owns 1-2 services, deploys independently. Deployment time: 10 minutes per service. But we introduced network latency (service-to-service calls), eventual consistency (inventory sync delays), and debugging complexity (distributed tracing required). Team velocity increased 40%, operational overhead doubled.

**Rule of thumb:** Microservices solve organizational scaling problems, not technical ones. Don't adopt until you have 20+ developers or deployment coordination is blocking releases.
