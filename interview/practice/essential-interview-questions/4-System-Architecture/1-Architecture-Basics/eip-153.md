## 153. What are the advantages of microservices?

**Microservices provide independent deployment, team autonomy, technology diversity, and failure isolation — at the cost of distributed system complexity.**

The benefits are organizational, not technical.

**Key advantages:**
- **Independent deployment** — change one service without redeploying everything
- **Team autonomy** — teams own services end-to-end, no coordination required
- **Technology diversity** — use the right tool for each job (Java for transactions, Python for ML, Go for high-throughput)
- **Failure isolation** — one service down doesn't crash the entire system
- **Independent scaling** — scale only hot services, not the entire application

**Trade-off:**
- Faster development — teams move at their own pace
- But: network calls replace method calls — latency, failures, eventual consistency
- Operational complexity — 10 services need 10x monitoring, logging, alerting infrastructure

**Real-world example:**
Our payment service needed PCI compliance — monolith meant entire codebase audited. Split payment into separate microservice — audit scope reduced 90%, compliance achieved in 2 months vs. estimated 12. Pricing service used complex algorithms — switched from Java to Python (better ML libraries) without affecting other services. During Black Friday, only checkout and inventory scaled (10 → 50 instances), catalog stayed at 5 instances. Saved 70% on infrastructure costs vs. scaling entire monolith.

**Rule of thumb:** Microservices shine when you need regulatory isolation, technology diversity, or independent scaling. For simple applications, the complexity isn't worth it.
