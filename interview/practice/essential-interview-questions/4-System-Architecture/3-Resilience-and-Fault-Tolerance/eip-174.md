## 174. What is bulkhead pattern?

**Bulkhead pattern isolates resources (thread pools, connections) by service or function — preventing one failing service from exhausting all resources and taking down the entire application.**

Named after ship bulkheads that contain flooding to one compartment.

**How it works:**
- Separate thread pools per service — inventory has 50 threads, payment has 50 threads
- If inventory service hangs, only its 50 threads exhaust — payment still has 50 threads available
- Failure contained — one service doesn't bring down everything

**Trade-off:**
- Resource isolation — failures don't cascade
- But: resource fragmentation — idle threads in one pool, starving in another
- More complex — manage multiple thread pools, tune sizes independently

**Real-world example:**
Our e-commerce platform had 200-thread pool shared across all services. Inventory service hung (database lock), all 200 threads waited. Payment, shipping, notifications — all blocked. Entire site down for 20 minutes. Implemented bulkheads: inventory (50 threads), payment (50), shipping (30), notifications (30), misc (40). Next inventory hang: 50 threads blocked, payment/shipping continued normally. 70% of site functionality preserved. Revenue loss: $50K → $5K.

**Rule of thumb:** Bulkhead by service criticality — payment gets more threads than notifications. Monitor thread pool utilization — alert at 80%. Use semaphore bulkheads for connection limits.
