## 190. What is backpressure?

**Backpressure is a flow control mechanism where downstream services signal upstream to slow down — preventing overload and cascading failures.**

It's about saying "I'm at capacity, slow down" instead of "I'll try to handle everything and fail."

**Implementation patterns:**
- **Pull-based** — consumer requests work when ready (natural backpressure)
- **Queue limits** — queue full, reject new work (explicit backpressure)
- **Rate limiting** — upstream limited to N requests/second (proactive backpressure)
- **Circuit breaker** — downstream unhealthy, upstream stops sending (reactive backpressure)

**Trade-off:**
- Prevents overload — system stays stable under load
- But: rejected requests — users get "try again later" errors
- Requires coordination — upstream must respect backpressure signals

**Real-world example:**
Our order processing: orders published to queue, consumers process asynchronously. Black Friday: 10K orders/minute, consumers handle 2K/minute. Queue grew to 100K orders, processing lagged 50 minutes. No backpressure — orders kept coming. Added queue limit (10K messages), reject orders when full. Next sale: queue hit 10K, rejected 20% of orders with "high traffic, try again" message. Processing stayed under 5 minutes. Rejected orders: 80% succeeded on retry. Better than accepting all and failing all.

**Rule of thumb:** Backpressure for async processing (queues, streams). Set limits based on consumer capacity, not producer rate. Reject early with clear error messages — "try again in 30 seconds." Monitor queue depth — alert at 80% capacity.
