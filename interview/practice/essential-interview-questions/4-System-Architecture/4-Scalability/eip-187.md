## 187. What is auto-scaling?

**Auto-scaling automatically adds or removes compute resources based on demand — handling traffic spikes without manual intervention.**

It's about paying for what you use, not over-provisioning for peak.

**Scaling triggers:**
- **CPU utilization** — scale up at 70%, scale down at 30% (most common)
- **Request count** — scale based on requests per second (better for I/O-bound apps)
- **Queue depth** — scale based on messages in queue (for batch processors)
- **Schedule** — scale up at 9 AM Monday, scale down at 6 PM Friday (predictable patterns)

**Trade-off:**
- Cost savings — 70% reduction vs. always-on peak capacity
- But: cold start latency — new instances take 2-5 minutes to warm up
- Thrashing — scaling up and down rapidly if thresholds too sensitive

**Real-world example:**
Our e-commerce site: Black Friday traffic 10x normal. Manually scaled to 50 instances night before. Traffic was 5x, not 10x — 25 idle instances, $10K wasted. Implemented auto-scaling: scale up at 70% CPU, scale down at 30% after 10 minutes. Black Friday: scaled from 10 → 45 instances over 2 hours, back to 15 after sale. Cost: $3K vs. $10K manual. But cold start caused 30-second delays for first 5 minutes — added warm-up script (preheat cache, connections). Zero delays since.

**Rule of thumb:** Auto-scaling for variable traffic. Scale up threshold: 60-70%. Scale down: 30% with 10-minute cooldown. Warm-up new instances before routing traffic. Use predictive scaling for known events (sales, launches).
