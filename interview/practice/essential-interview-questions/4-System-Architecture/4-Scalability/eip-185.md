## 185. What is caching strategy?

**Caching strategy determines what to cache, where to cache, when to invalidate — balancing freshness against performance.**

Wrong strategy causes stale data, cache thrashing, or no performance benefit.

**Common patterns:**
- **Cache-aside (lazy loading)** — app checks cache, misses → load from DB, populate cache (simple, stale data possible)
- **Write-through** — app writes to cache, cache writes to DB (strong consistency, slower writes)
- **Write-behind** — app writes to cache, cache async writes to DB (fast writes, risk of data loss)
- **Refresh-ahead** — cache refreshes before expiration (no stale reads, but complex)

**Trade-off:**
- Cache-aside: simple, but stale data until next read
- Write-through: consistent, but write latency = cache + DB
- TTL: simple invalidation, but data may be stale for TTL duration

**Real-world example:**
Our product catalog: cache-aside with 1-hour TTL. Price changes: updated in DB, cache showed old price for up to 1 hour. 500 customers complained, charged wrong prices. Switched to write-through for price updates, cache-aside for product details. Price changes: instant in cache. Product details: 1-hour TTL acceptable. But write-through slowed price updates 2x (cache + DB). Added async cache invalidation: update DB, publish event, cache invalidates asynchronously. Best of both: fast writes, fresh data.

**Rule of thumb:** Cache-aside for read-heavy, tolerance for staleness (product details). Write-through for critical data (prices, inventory). TTL for simplicity, explicit invalidation for freshness.
