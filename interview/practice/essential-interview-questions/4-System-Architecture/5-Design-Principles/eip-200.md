## 200. What is distributed tracing?

**Distributed tracing tracks requests as they flow through multiple services — correlating logs, measuring latency per service, identifying bottlenecks in microservices architectures.**

It's the difference between "something is slow" and "the payment service is slow, specifically the fraud check."

**Key concepts:**
- **Trace** — end-to-end journey of a request (order placement)
- **Span** — individual operation within a trace (payment authorization)
- **Trace ID** — unique identifier propagated across all services
- **Parent/child spans** — hierarchy of operations

**Trade-off:**
- Visibility — see exactly where time is spent, which service failed
- But: overhead — 5-10% performance cost for trace collection
- Data volume — 1M requests/day = 100MB+ of trace data

**Real-world example:**
Our checkout: 8 microservices, latency spiked to 5 seconds. No tracing — checked logs of all 8 services, couldn't correlate. Took 4 hours to find: inventory service waiting on external supplier API (4-second timeout). Implemented Jaeger distributed tracing: each request has trace ID, spans show time per service. Next latency spike: trace showed payment service → fraud check → external API, 3.5 seconds in fraud check. Fixed in 30 minutes (added timeout, fallback). Now we sample 10% of requests (reduce overhead), keep 100% of errors. Mean time to resolution: 4 hours → 15 minutes.

**Rule of thumb:** Distributed tracing for any microservices architecture (3+ services). Sample traces (1-10%) to reduce overhead. Always trace errors (100%). Propagate trace ID in all headers — logs, messages, external calls.
