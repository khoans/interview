## 197. What is eventual consistency?

**Eventual consistency means that after an update, the system will converge to a consistent state over time — but reads may return stale data temporarily.**

It's the trade-off for high availability and low latency in distributed systems.

**When it's acceptable:**
- Social media — seeing a post 5 seconds late is fine
- Shopping cart — items sync across devices eventually
- Analytics — dashboards can be minutes behind

**When it's NOT acceptable:**
- Banking — account balance must be accurate immediately
- Inventory — can't sell items already sold
- Authentication — revoked access must take effect immediately

**Trade-off:**
- High availability — system works even when some nodes are unreachable
- Low latency — no waiting for all replicas to sync
- But: stale reads — users may see outdated data
- Complexity — handling conflicts, reconciliation

**Real-world example:**
Our social platform: posts replicated to 5 data centers. User posts in US, friend in Europe sees it 3 seconds later (replication lag). Acceptable — social content is eventually consistent. But comments on posts: user comments, doesn't see their own comment (read from different replica). Unacceptable. Fixed: user's own actions read from primary (strong consistency), others from replicas (eventual consistency). Zero "where's my comment" tickets since.

**Rule of thumb:** Eventual consistency for read-heavy, latency-sensitive, non-critical data. Strong consistency for financial, inventory, security-critical data. Hybrid approach: user's own data strong, others eventual.
