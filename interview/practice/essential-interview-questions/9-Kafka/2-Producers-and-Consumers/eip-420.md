## 420. What is consumer offset?

**Consumer offset is the position of consumer in partition — tracks which messages have been processed.**

It's how Kafka knows where to resume — after restart, rebalance, or failure.

**How it works:**
- Consumer reads message at offset N
- Processes message
- Commits offset N+1 — "I've processed up to N"
- On restart, resumes from offset N+1

**Trade-off:**
- Commit after every message — safe, but overhead
- Commit after batch — efficient, but may reprocess on failure
- Auto-commit vs. manual — convenience vs. control

**Real-world example:**
Our consumer auto-committed every 5 seconds — efficient. Consumer crashed 3 seconds after last commit — 3 seconds of messages reprocessed on restart. Duplicate processing: 5% of messages. Switched to manual commit after each message — no duplicates. But throughput dropped 30% — commit overhead. Compromise: commit after every 10 messages — duplicates: 5% → 0.5%. Throughput: recovered 90%.

**Rule of thumb:** Manual commit for critical processing — commit after processing, not before. Batch commits for throughput — every 10-100 messages. Idempotent processing — handle duplicates gracefully.
