## 413. What is a consumer group?

**A consumer group is a set of consumers that jointly consume messages — each partition is consumed by exactly one consumer in the group.**

It's how Kafka scales consumption — add consumers to group, partitions are rebalanced.

**Key characteristics:**
- **Parallelism** — N consumers = N partitions consumed in parallel
- **Load balancing** — partitions distributed across consumers
- **Fault tolerance** — if consumer fails, partitions reassigned to survivors
- **One consumer per partition** — partition not consumed twice within group

**Trade-off:**
- Scalability — add consumers to scale throughput
- But: rebalancing — adding/removing consumers causes brief pause
- Consumer group limit — can't have more consumers than partitions

**Real-world example:**
Our order processor had single consumer — 100 orders/second. Traffic grew to 1000 orders/second, consumer lagged. Added 9 consumers to same consumer group — 10 consumers total, 10 partitions. Throughput: 100 → 1000 orders/second. But adding consumers triggered rebalance — 30-second pause, messages not processed. Optimized: static membership — consumers have stable IDs, rebalance only on actual failures. Rebalance: 30 seconds → 5 seconds.

**Rule of thumb:** One consumer group per processing logic. Consumers = partitions for max parallelism. Static membership for stable consumer sets — reduce rebalance frequency.
