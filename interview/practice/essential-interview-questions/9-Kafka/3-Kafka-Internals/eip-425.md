## 425. What is a follower partition?

**A follower partition is a replica that replicates from leader — provides redundancy, takes over on leader failure.**

Followers don't serve traffic (normally) — they replicate and stand by.

**Responsibilities:**
- **Replicate** — fetch messages from leader, write to local disk
- **Stay in-sync** — keep up with leader (part of ISR)
- **Ready for failover** — become leader if current leader fails

**Trade-off:**
- Redundancy — data survives leader failure
- But: followers are passive — don't serve reads (unless configured)
- Replication lag — followers may lag behind leader

**Real-world example:**
Our 3-replica topic had 1 leader, 2 followers. Follower 1 was slow — network issue, lagged 1000 messages behind. Leader failed — slow follower became leader, consumers saw 1000 missing messages (data loss risk). Fixed: min.insync.replicas=2 — producer waits for 2 replicas. Slow follower excluded from ISR — can't become leader until caught up. Data loss: prevented. Also enabled follower fetching — followers serve read traffic, reduce leader load. Leader CPU: 80% → 40%.

**Rule of thumb:** Minimum 2 followers — tolerate 1 failure. Monitor replication lag — alert if follower falls behind. min.insync.replicas for durability — prevent writes when ISR is small.
