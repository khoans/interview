## 428. What is rebalancing in Kafka?

**Rebalancing is the process of redistributing partitions among consumers in a consumer group.**

It happens when consumers join/leave — partitions reassigned for load balancing.

**When it occurs:**
- New consumer joins group
- Consumer leaves (crash, shutdown)
- Partitions added to topic
- Consumer timeout (missed heartbeat)

**Trade-off:**
- Load balancing — partitions evenly distributed
- But: stop-the-world — consumers stop processing during rebalance
- Frequency — frequent rebalances hurt throughput

**Real-world example:**
Our consumer group had 10 consumers — frequent rebalances (every 5 minutes). Root cause: consumers took 6 minutes to process batch, missed heartbeat (3 minute timeout), kicked from group, rebalance triggered. Vicious cycle. Fixed: increased session.timeout.ms to 10 minutes, processing time < 6 minutes. Rebalances: every 5 minutes → once per day (actual consumer changes). Also implemented cooperative rebalancing — incremental rebalance, not stop-the-world. Rebalance impact: 30-second pause → 5-second gradual shift.

**Rule of thumb:** Tune heartbeat/timeout — processing time < session timeout. Cooperative rebalancing — minimize disruption. Monitor rebalance frequency — alert on unexpected rebalances.
