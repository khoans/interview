## 424. What is a leader partition?

**A leader partition is the primary replica that handles all reads and writes for a partition.**

Followers replicate from leader — leader is the source of truth.

**Responsibilities:**
- **Handle produces** — accept incoming messages from producers
- **Handle consumes** — serve messages to consumers
- **Replicate to followers** — send messages to follower replicas
- **Manage ISR** — track which followers are in-sync

**Trade-off:**
- Single leader — simple consistency model
- But: leader is bottleneck — all traffic goes through leader
- Leader failure — requires election, brief unavailability

**Real-world example:**
Our hot topic had 1 leader partition — all traffic to single broker. Broker CPU hit 100%, latency spiked 10ms → 200ms. Added more partitions — 10 partitions, 10 leaders across 10 brokers. Traffic distributed — per-broker CPU: 100% → 10%. Latency: 200ms → 15ms. But leader election during broker maintenance caused 5-second blip. Optimized: preferred leader election — leaders on specific brokers, controlled failover. Blip: 5 seconds → 500ms.

**Rule of thumb:** Multiple partitions for hot topics — distribute leaders across brokers. Monitor leader distribution — ensure even spread. Plan leader election — controlled failover during maintenance.
