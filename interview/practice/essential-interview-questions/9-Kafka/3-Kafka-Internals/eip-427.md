## 427. How does Kafka handle broker failures?

**Kafka handles broker failures through replication — followers take over when leader fails, automatic leader election.**

It's automatic failover — no manual intervention required.

**Failure handling:**
- **Detection** — ZooKeeper/KRaft detects broker failure (10 seconds)
- **Leader election** — new leader elected from ISR (seconds)
- **Consumer reconnection** — consumers reconnect to new leader
- **Producer reconnection** — producers reconnect, retry in-flight messages

**Trade-off:**
- Automatic failover — no manual intervention
- But: brief disruption — seconds of unavailability during election
- Uncommitted messages — may be lost if not replicated

**Real-world example:**
Our 5-broker cluster had broker failure during peak traffic. Detection: 10 seconds. Leader election: 5 seconds. Total disruption: 15 seconds. Messages in-flight: 100 messages lost (acks=1, not replicated). Post-mortem: changed to acks=all — all messages replicated before ack. Message loss: eliminated. Also added unclean.leader.election.enable=false — only ISR members can become leader. Prevents data loss from out-of-sync follower becoming leader.

**Rule of thumb:** Replication factor 3 — tolerate 1 failure. acks=all for critical data — ensure replication. unclean.leader.election.enable=false — prevent data loss. Monitor broker health — predict failures before they happen.
