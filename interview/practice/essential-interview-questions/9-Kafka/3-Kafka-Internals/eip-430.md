## 430. What is the role of group coordinator?

**Group coordinator is a broker that manages consumer group state — membership, partition assignments, offsets.**

Each consumer group has one coordinator — central point for group management.

**Responsibilities:**
- **Track membership** — which consumers are in group
- **Manage rebalancing** — assign partitions to consumers
- **Store offsets** — consumer commit offsets to coordinator
- **Heartbeat handling** — detect dead consumers

**Trade-off:**
- Centralized coordination — simple group management
- But: coordinator is SPOF — if coordinator fails, group management paused
- Coordinator failover — another broker takes over

**Real-world example:**
Our Kafka cluster had 100 consumer groups — all coordinated by single broker (broker 1). Broker 1 failed — all 100 groups couldn't rebalance, couldn't commit offsets. 2-minute disruption while new coordinator elected. Fixed: consumer groups distributed across brokers — group coordinator based on group.id hash. Load: evenly distributed. Single broker failure: affects 10% of groups, not 100%. Also monitored coordinator load — hot groups (high rebalance frequency) identified, split to separate groups.

**Rule of thumb:** Consumer groups distributed across brokers — not all on same coordinator. Monitor coordinator load — rebalance frequency, offset commit rate. Hot groups — split if coordinator overloaded.
