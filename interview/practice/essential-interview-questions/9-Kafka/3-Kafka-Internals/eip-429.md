## 429. When does consumer rebalancing occur?

**Consumer rebalancing occurs when group membership changes — consumer joins, leaves, or is deemed dead.**

Also on partition changes — new partitions added to topic.

**Triggers:**
- **Consumer join** — new consumer starts, needs partitions
- **Consumer leave** — consumer shuts down gracefully
- **Consumer failure** — missed heartbeats, removed from group
- **Partition change** — topic partitions increased

**Trade-off:**
- Automatic rebalancing — no manual partition assignment
- But: disruption — consumers stop during rebalance
- Eager vs. cooperative — stop-the-world vs. incremental

**Real-world example:**
Our deployment caused mass rebalancing — rolled restart of 10 consumers. All consumers left simultaneously, rejoined one-by-one. 10 rebalances in 10 minutes — processing halted for 5 minutes total. Fixed: blue-green deployment — deploy 5 new consumers, wait for stable, decommission 5 old. Rebalances: 10 → 2. Downtime: 5 minutes → 30 seconds. Also switched to cooperative rebalancing — only changed partitions reassigned, not all. Rebalance time: 30 seconds → 10 seconds.

**Rule of thumb:** Graceful shutdown — consumers leave group cleanly, trigger single rebalance. Blue-green deployment — avoid mass restarts. Cooperative rebalancing — minimize partition movement.
