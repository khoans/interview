## 423. What is the retention policy in Kafka?

**Retention policy controls how long messages are kept before deletion — time-based or size-based.**

It's storage management — prevent unbounded growth.

**Policy types:**
- **Time-based** — delete messages older than N hours/days
- **Size-based** — delete when partition exceeds N GB
- **Combined** — whichever limit hit first

**Trade-off:**
- Longer retention — consumers can reprocess historical data
- But: storage cost — more disk space required
- Too short — consumers can't catch up after outage

**Real-world example:**
Our default retention was 7 days — worked until consumer bug caused 10-day lag. Consumer restarted — missed 3 days of messages (already deleted). Data loss: 3 days of orders. Increased retention to 30 days — storage cost +300%. But consumer lag incident: consumer had 20-day lag, caught up successfully. Also added size-based retention — 100GB per partition max. Prevents runaway storage from high-volume topics.

**Rule of thumb:** Retention based on max expected consumer lag — 2x safety margin. 7 days minimum — allows weekend outage recovery. Size-based limit — prevent storage exhaustion. Monitor lag — alert before retention boundary.
