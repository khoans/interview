## 401. What is Apache Kafka?

**Kafka is a distributed event streaming platform — handles high-throughput, fault-tolerant message streaming in real-time.**

It's not just a message queue — it's a durable log that stores streams of events.

**Key characteristics:**
- High throughput — millions of messages per second
- Durable — messages persisted to disk, replicated
- Scalable — add brokers to scale horizontally
- Real-time — sub-second latency from produce to consume

**Trade-off:**
- Throughput over latency — optimized for batch, not low-latency RPC
- Complexity — distributed system requires operational expertise
- Overkill for simple use cases — RabbitMQ may suffice for basic queuing

**Real-world example:**
Our e-commerce had 10 microservices with point-to-point RabbitMQ connections. Message routing was spaghetti — 50+ queues, complex routing rules. Service addition required updating 10 producers. Migrated to Kafka — services publish to topics, consumers subscribe. New service: just subscribe to existing topics. Throughput: 10K msg/s → 500K msg/s. But operational complexity increased — needed dedicated Kafka team, monitoring, capacity planning.

**Rule of thumb:** Kafka for high-throughput event streaming, event sourcing, CQRS. RabbitMQ for simple request-response, task queues. Don't use Kafka for RPC — use gRPC or REST.
