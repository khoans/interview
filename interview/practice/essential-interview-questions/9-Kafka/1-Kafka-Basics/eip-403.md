## 403. What are the main components of Kafka?

**Kafka has four main components: Topics (categories), Producers (publishers), Consumers (subscribers), Brokers (servers).**

Plus ZooKeeper for coordination (legacy) or KRaft (newer versions).

**Components:**
- **Topics** — categories for messages, like database tables
- **Producers** — applications that publish messages to topics
- **Consumers** — applications that subscribe and process messages
- **Brokers** — Kafka servers that store and serve messages
- **ZooKeeper/KRaft** — cluster coordination, metadata management

**Trade-off:**
- Simple abstraction — topics/producers/consumers easy to understand
- But: operational complexity — managing brokers, partitions, replication
- ZooKeeper dependency — additional system to manage (being phased out)

**Real-world example:**
Our team started with single-broker Kafka — worked for development. Production: single point of failure, broker crashed, 4-hour outage. Added 2 more brokers (3-node cluster), replication factor 3. Broker failure: zero downtime, automatic failover. But ZooKeeper became bottleneck — single ZK cluster for 10 Kafka clusters. Migrated to KRaft (Kafka Raft) — removed ZooKeeper dependency. Operational overhead: reduced 30%.

**Rule of thumb:** Minimum 3 brokers for production — tolerate 1 failure. Replication factor 3 — data survives 2 broker failures. Plan ZooKeeper/KRaft capacity — coordination is critical path.
