## 407. What is a Kafka broker?

**A broker is a Kafka server — stores messages, serves producers and consumers, manages partitions.**

A Kafka cluster is multiple brokers working together.

**Responsibilities:**
- Store messages — persist to disk, replicate to followers
- Serve producers — accept incoming messages
- Serve consumers — deliver messages to subscribers
- Manage partitions — leader election, ISR tracking

**Trade-off:**
- More brokers — more capacity, more fault tolerance
- But: operational complexity — monitoring, upgrades, capacity planning
- Broker failure — automatic failover, but brief disruption

**Real-world example:**
Our 3-broker cluster handled 10K msg/s smoothly. Added new feature — 100K msg/s. Brokers hit 90% disk, latency spiked 10ms → 500ms. Added 6 brokers (9 total), rebalanced partitions. Latency: 500ms → 15ms. But broker upgrade required rolling restart — 9 brokers × 10 minutes = 90 minutes. Optimized: blue-green upgrade — spin up new cluster, switch traffic, decommission old. Downtime: 90 minutes → 30 seconds (traffic switch).

**Rule of thumb:** 3 brokers minimum for production — tolerate 1 failure. Monitor disk usage — alert at 70%. Rolling upgrades for zero downtime — upgrade one broker at a time.
