## 408. What is a Kafka cluster?

**A Kafka cluster is a group of brokers working together — provides fault tolerance, horizontal scalability.**

Single broker = single point of failure. Cluster = high availability.

**Key characteristics:**
- Multiple brokers — typically 3, 5, or 7 (odd numbers for quorum)
- Shared metadata — topics, partitions, replication tracked centrally
- Automatic failover — if leader broker fails, follower becomes leader
- Load balancing — partitions distributed across brokers

**Trade-off:**
- Fault tolerance — cluster survives broker failures
- But: coordination overhead — brokers must coordinate leadership, replication
- Minimum 3 brokers — cost vs. availability trade-off

**Real-world example:**
Our startup began with single-broker Kafka — cheap, simple. Broker disk failed — 8-hour outage, data loss. Post-mortem: migrated to 5-broker cluster, replication factor 3. Next disk failure: automatic failover, zero data loss, 30-second blip. Cost: 5x broker cost. Value: zero outages in 18 months. Also, cluster grew to 20 brokers — single ZooKeeper cluster became bottleneck. Split into 4 Kafka clusters (5 brokers each) — isolation, better performance.

**Rule of thumb:** Minimum 3 brokers for production — tolerate 1 failure. Replication factor 3 — data survives 2 failures. Split into multiple clusters at scale — isolation, blast radius reduction.
