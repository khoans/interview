## 406. Why do we need partitions in Kafka?

**Partitions enable parallelism — multiple consumers process different partitions simultaneously, increasing throughput.**

Without partitions, single consumer is bottleneck — can't scale beyond single machine's capacity.

**Benefits:**
- **Parallelism** — N partitions = N consumers processing simultaneously
- **Scalability** — add partitions to scale throughput
- **Distribution** — partitions spread across brokers, no single point of failure

**Trade-off:**
- Throughput — more partitions = higher throughput
- But: ordering limited to partition — can't order across partitions
- Partition management — rebalancing when partitions change

**Real-world example:**
Our payment processing had single partition — 100 payments/second, single consumer. Peak traffic: 1000 payments/second, consumer lagged 10 minutes. Added 10 partitions — 10 consumers processed in parallel. Throughput: 100 → 1000 payments/second. Lag: 10 minutes → 0. But payment updates went to random partitions — payment created then updated arrived out of order. Fixed: partition by payment_id — same payment always same partition, ordering preserved.

**Rule of thumb:** Partitions for throughput scaling. Partition by business key for ordering. Plan partition count upfront — increasing partitions later requires rebalancing.
