## 409. What is ZooKeeper? What is its role in Kafka?

**ZooKeeper is a distributed coordination service — Kafka uses it for metadata management, leader election, cluster coordination.**

Kafka 3.0+ introduces KRaft mode — removes ZooKeeper dependency.

**ZooKeeper's role:**
- **Metadata storage** — topics, partitions, broker info
- **Leader election** — elect partition leaders, controller broker
- **Cluster coordination** — detect broker failures, trigger rebalancing
- **Configuration storage** — ACLs, quotas, topic configs

**Trade-off:**
- Proven coordination — ZooKeeper is battle-tested
- But: operational overhead — another distributed system to manage
- ZooKeeper bottleneck — single ZK cluster for multiple Kafka clusters
- KRaft alternative — simpler, Kafka-native coordination

**Real-world example:**
Our 10 Kafka clusters shared single ZooKeeper cluster — ZK became SPOF. ZK outage: all 10 Kafka clusters couldn't rebalance, couldn't elect leaders. 2-hour outage. Split to dedicated ZK per Kafka cluster — isolation, but 10 ZK clusters to manage. Migrated to KRaft mode — no ZooKeeper, Kafka manages own metadata. Operational overhead: 10 ZK clusters eliminated. KRaft adoption: 6 months (testing, migration). Zero ZK-related outages since.

**Rule of thumb:** ZooKeeper for Kafka <3.0 — dedicated ZK per Kafka cluster. KRaft for Kafka 3.0+ — simpler, fewer moving parts. Plan KRaft migration — test thoroughly before production.
