## 438. What is message loss in Kafka? How do you prevent it?

**Message loss occurs when messages are produced but not consumed — producer failures, broker failures, consumer misconfiguration.**

Prevention: proper configuration at every layer.

**Loss points:**
- **Producer** — acks=0, no retries — message lost on network failure
- **Broker** — unclean leader election — messages not replicated lost
- **Consumer** — auto-commit before processing — offset committed, message not processed
- **Retention** — consumer lag exceeds retention — messages deleted before consumed

**Prevention:**
- Producer: acks=all, retries > 0, enable.idempotence=true
- Broker: min.insync.replicas=2, unclean.leader.election.enable=false
- Consumer: manual commit after processing, monitor lag
- Retention: retention > max expected lag

**Real-world example:**
Our order system had message loss — 0.1% orders lost. Root cause analysis: 40% producer (acks=1, leader failure), 40% consumer (auto-commit before processing), 20% retention (lag exceeded 7-day retention). Fixes: acks=all, manual commit, retention 7 → 30 days. Message loss: 0.1% → 0%. Also added end-to-end tracking — each order tracked from produce to consume. Loss detected within minutes, not days.

**Rule of thumb:** acks=all for producers — wait for replication. Manual commit for consumers — commit after processing. Retention > max lag — 30 days minimum. End-to-end tracking — detect loss quickly.
