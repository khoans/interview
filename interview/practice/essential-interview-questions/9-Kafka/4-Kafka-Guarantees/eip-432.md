## 432. How does Kafka ensure durability?

**Kafka ensures durability through replication — messages replicated to multiple brokers, acks configuration controls when message is considered committed.**

Durability = messages survive broker failures.

**Mechanisms:**
- **Replication** — multiple copies across brokers
- **acks=all** — producer waits for all ISR replicas
- **min.insync.replicas** — minimum replicas for write
- **Unclean election disabled** — only ISR can become leader

**Trade-off:**
- Full durability — acks=all, min.insync.replicas=2
- But: higher latency — wait for replication
- Reduced availability — writes rejected if ISR too small

**Real-world example:**
Our financial transactions used acks=1 — leader acknowledges, followers replicate async. Leader failed before replication — 100 transactions lost. Financial impact: $100K reconciliation cost. Changed to acks=all, min.insync.replicas=2. Transaction loss: eliminated. Latency: 5ms → 15ms. For financial data, 15ms is acceptable. Also disabled unclean.leader.election — out-of-sync follower can't become leader. Prevents data loss from stale leader.

**Rule of thumb:** acks=all for durable writes — wait for replication. min.insync.replicas=2 — require 2 replicas. unclean.leader.election.enable=false — prevent stale leader. Monitor ISR — alert when below threshold.
