## 434. What is at-most-once delivery?

**At-most-once delivery means messages may be lost, but never duplicated — fire-and-forget.**

It's the weakest guarantee — fastest, but data loss possible.

**How to achieve:**
- Producer: acks=0 — no acknowledgment
- Consumer: auto-commit before processing — offset committed, message may not be processed

**Trade-off:**
- Fastest — no acknowledgment overhead
- But: message loss on any failure — producer retry, broker failure, consumer crash
- Use case: metrics, logs — occasional loss acceptable

**Real-world example:**
Our metrics pipeline used at-most-once — acks=0, auto-commit. 1M metrics/hour, 0.1% loss (1000 metrics/hour). Business accepted — metrics are approximate, trends matter more than exact values. But during broker failure, 10 minutes of metrics lost (10K metrics). Alerting gap — didn't notice performance degradation for 1 hour. Upgraded to at-least-once for alerting metrics — no loss for critical alerts. Non-critical metrics: kept at-most-once.

**Rule of thumb:** At-most-once for disposable data — metrics, debug logs. Not for business-critical data — orders, payments. Upgrade critical streams to at-least-once.
