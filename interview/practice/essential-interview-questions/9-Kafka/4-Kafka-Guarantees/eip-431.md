## 431. What guarantees does Kafka provide? What if all the consumer services process the message fail?

**Kafka provides: durability (messages persisted), ordering (within partition), at-least-once delivery (with proper config).**

If all consumers fail: messages remain in Kafka, processed when consumers recover.

**Guarantees:**
- **Durability** — messages replicated, survive broker failures
- **Ordering** — within partition, strict ordering
- **At-least-once** — with acks=all, idempotent producer, manual commit

**Consumer failure scenario:**
- All consumers crash — messages not consumed
- Messages remain in Kafka — retention policy applies
- Consumers restart — resume from last committed offset
- No data loss — messages still in Kafka

**Trade-off:**
- Durability — messages survive consumer failures
- But: retention limit — messages deleted after N days
- Consumer lag — if consumers down too long, messages may expire

**Real-world example:**
Our consumer service had bug — crashed on specific message type. All 10 consumers crashed in loop. Messages accumulated in Kafka — 1M messages, 2-day backlog. Fixed consumer bug, restarted. Consumers caught up in 4 hours — processed backlog. No data loss (retention was 7 days, backlog was 2 days). But alerting was missing — nobody noticed 2-day gap. Added consumer lag monitoring — alert if lag > 1 hour. Now caught within 1 hour, not 2 days.

**Rule of thumb:** Retention > max expected consumer downtime — 7 days minimum. Consumer lag monitoring — alert before retention boundary. Idempotent consumers — handle reprocessing after crash.
