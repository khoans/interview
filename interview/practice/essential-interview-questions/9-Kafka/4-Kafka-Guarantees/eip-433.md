## 433. How does Kafka ensure availability?

**Kafka ensures availability through replication and automatic failover — if leader fails, follower becomes leader, consumers reconnect.**

Availability = system remains operational despite failures.

**Mechanisms:**
- **Replication** — multiple copies, survive broker failures
- **Automatic leader election** — new leader within seconds
- **Consumer reconnection** — consumers reconnect to new leader
- **Multiple brokers** — cluster survives individual broker failures

**Trade-off:**
- High availability — 99.9%+ uptime with proper setup
- But: brief disruption during failover — seconds
- Consistency vs. availability — acks=all may reject writes during partition

**Real-world example:**
Our 3-broker cluster had 99.9% availability target. Broker failure: automatic failover, 5-second blip. Met availability target. But network partition — 2 brokers isolated from 1. acks=all rejected writes (ISR < 2). Availability: dropped during partition. Business decision: changed to acks=1 for non-critical topics — accept writes with single replica. Availability: 99.9% → 99.99%. Critical topics kept acks=all — durability over availability.

**Rule of thumb:** 3+ brokers for high availability — tolerate 1 failure. acks=1 for availability-critical, acks=all for durability-critical. Monitor availability — track uptime, failover frequency.
