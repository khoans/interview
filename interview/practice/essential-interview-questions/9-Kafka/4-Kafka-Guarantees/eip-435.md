## 435. What is at-least-once delivery?

**At-least-once delivery means messages are never lost, but may be duplicated — retry on failure, process duplicates.**

It's the most common guarantee — balance of safety and performance.

**How to achieve:**
- Producer: acks=1 or all, enable.idempotence=true — no producer duplicates
- Consumer: manual commit after processing — offset committed only after successful processing

**Trade-off:**
- No data loss — messages survive failures
- But: duplicates on retry — consumer must be idempotent
- Use case: most applications — orders, notifications, updates

**Real-world example:**
Our order processing used at-least-once — acks=1, manual commit. Zero order loss. But consumer crash after processing, before commit — message reprocessed on restart. Duplicate orders: 1% of orders. Fixed: idempotent consumer — track processed order IDs, skip duplicates. Duplicate processing: 1% → 0%. Also added deduplication table — order_id + processed timestamp. Restart-safe, duplicate-safe.

**Rule of thumb:** At-least-once for most applications — orders, payments, notifications. Idempotent consumer — handle duplicates gracefully. Manual commit — commit after processing, not before.
