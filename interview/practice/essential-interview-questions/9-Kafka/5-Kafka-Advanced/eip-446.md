## 446. How do you monitor Kafka?

**Kafka monitoring requires broker metrics, consumer lag, topic health, and cluster-wide dashboards.**

Key tools: Prometheus + Grafana, Confluent Control Center, Kafka Manager.

**What to monitor:**
- **Broker** — CPU, disk, network, request latency
- **Consumer lag** — messages behind, lag growth rate
- **Topic** — partition count, replication factor, ISR health
- **Cluster** — controller status, under-replicated partitions

**Trade-off:**
- Comprehensive monitoring — catch issues early
- But: alert fatigue — too many alerts, ignored
- Cost — monitoring infrastructure overhead

**Real-world example:**
Our Kafka had no monitoring — broker disk filled, 4-hour outage. Post-mortem: implemented Prometheus + Grafana. Dashboards: broker health, consumer lag, topic metrics. Alerts: disk > 70%, lag > 1 hour, under-replicated partitions > 0. Next disk issue: alerted at 70%, expanded disk before full. Zero outages since. But 50 alerts — alert fatigue. Tuned: 10 critical alerts (page on-call), 40 warning alerts (ticket). On-call load: manageable.

**Rule of thumb:** Monitor broker disk — alert at 70%. Consumer lag — alert before retention boundary. Under-replicated partitions — alert immediately. Tune alerts — critical vs. warning, avoid alert fatigue.
