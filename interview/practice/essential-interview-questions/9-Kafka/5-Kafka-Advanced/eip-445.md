## 445. What is multi-cluster replication?

**Multi-cluster replication is replicating Kafka topics across multiple Kafka clusters — for DR, geo-distribution, data locality.**

Patterns: active-passive (DR), active-active (geo-distribution).

**Use cases:**
- **Disaster recovery** — passive cluster in different region
- **Geo-distribution** — users read from nearest cluster
- **Data locality** — comply with data residency requirements
- **Migration** — replicate during cluster migration

**Trade-off:**
- DR — survive region outage
- But: replication lag — async, target may be stale
- Complexity — conflict resolution for active-active

**Real-world example:**
Our EU customers required data residency — EU data must stay in EU. US cluster replicated to EU cluster — EU customers read from EU cluster. Compliance: achieved. Replication: MirrorMaker 2, async, 2-second lag. But active-active conflict — same user updated in US and EU simultaneously. Conflict resolution: last-write-wins (timestamp-based). Conflicts: rare (0.01% of updates). Also added monitoring — cross-cluster lag, conflict rate. Alert on lag > 10 seconds, conflict spike.

**Rule of thumb:** Multi-cluster for DR and data residency. Active-passive simpler — no conflicts. Active-active requires conflict resolution — last-write-wins or business logic. Monitor replication lag and conflicts.
