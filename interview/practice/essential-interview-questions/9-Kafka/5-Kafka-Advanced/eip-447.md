## 447. What are important Kafka metrics?

**Important Kafka metrics: consumer lag, under-replicated partitions, broker disk usage, request latency, ISR shrink rate.**

These indicate health, performance, and impending issues.

**Critical metrics:**
- **Consumer lag** — messages behind, lag growth rate
- **Under-replicated partitions** — replication behind, data at risk
- **Broker disk usage** — running out of disk = outage
- **Request latency (p99)** — producer/consumer latency
- **ISR shrink rate** — followers falling behind

**Trade-off:**
- Focus on critical metrics — actionable alerts
- But: too few metrics — miss early warning signs
- Context matters — lag of 1000 is fine for batch, critical for real-time

**Real-world example:**
Our Kafka had 100+ metrics — overwhelming, missed critical issues. Reduced to 10 critical metrics — consumer lag, disk, ISR, latency. Alerts: actionable, on-call could diagnose in 5 minutes. But missed network saturation — not monitored. Added network metrics — saturation detected before it caused replication lag. Also added business metrics — messages produced/consumed per topic. Anomaly detection — 50% drop in production detected producer bug within minutes.

**Rule of thumb:** 10 critical metrics — consumer lag, disk, ISR, latency, network. Business metrics — production/consumption rates. Anomaly detection — detect deviations from normal. Actionable alerts — on-call can diagnose quickly.
