## 449. What is backpressure in Kafka?

**Backpressure is flow control — slow consumers signal producers to slow down, prevent overwhelming the system.**

Kafka doesn't have built-in backpressure — consumers pull at their own pace.

**How it works:**
- Consumer lag grows — consumer can't keep up
- Producer continues — lag grows unbounded
- Eventually: disk full, retention exceeded, messages lost

**Trade-off:**
- Kafka decoupling — producers don't know consumer state
- But: no backpressure — producers can overwhelm consumers
- Application-level backpressure needed — monitor lag, throttle producers

**Real-world example:**
Our producer generated 100K msg/s — consumer processed 10K msg/s. Lag grew: 1M → 10M → 100M messages. Disk filled — broker outage. No backpressure — producer didn't know consumer was drowning. Implemented lag monitoring — alert at 1 hour lag. Also added producer throttling — if lag > 2 hours, producer slows to consumer pace. Lag growth: stopped. Disk: stabilized. Also scaled consumers — 1 → 10 consumers, throughput matched producer.

**Rule of thumb:** Monitor consumer lag — alert before retention boundary. Producer throttling — slow down if lag exceeds threshold. Scale consumers — match producer throughput. Disk monitoring — prevent disk exhaustion.
