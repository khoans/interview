## 450. How do you handle poison pill messages in Kafka?

**Poison pill messages are messages that always fail processing — cause consumer to crash or loop indefinitely.**

Handling: detect, isolate (DLQ), continue processing.

**Strategies:**
- **Retry with limit** — retry N times, then give up
- **Dead letter queue** — send to DLQ topic after retries exhausted
- **Skip and log** — log error, skip message, continue
- **Circuit breaker** — if too many failures, pause consumer, alert

**Trade-off:**
- DLQ — isolate poison pills, main stream continues
- But: DLQ management — need process to handle DLQ
- Skip — don't lose entire stream, but message lost

**Real-world example:**
Our consumer crashed on specific message — deserialization failed (corrupt message). Consumer restarted, same message, crashed again. Infinite loop — 100 restarts/hour. Implemented: try-catch around processing, 3 retries, then send to DLQ. Poison pill: isolated to DLQ. Main stream: continued. DLQ process: investigated corrupt message — producer bug. Fixed producer, reprocessed DLQ (100 messages). Also added circuit breaker — if 10 failures in 1 minute, pause consumer, alert. Caught producer regression — 50 poison pills in 1 minute, alerted, fixed within 15 minutes.

**Rule of thumb:** Try-catch around processing — don't crash on bad message. Retry 3 times — transient failures recover. DLQ for poison pills — isolate, investigate. Circuit breaker — alert on failure spike. Monitor DLQ — process regularly.
