## 444. What is Kafka MirrorMaker?

**MirrorMaker is a tool for replicating Kafka topics between clusters — cross-cluster, cross-datacenter replication.**

It's for disaster recovery, multi-region deployments.

**How it works:**
- Source cluster — MirrorMaker consumes from source topics
- Replication — produces to target cluster
- Target cluster — mirror of source, async replication

**Trade-off:**
- Disaster recovery — target cluster is backup
- But: replication lag — async, target may lag behind source
- Active-passive — target is standby, not active (typically)

**Real-world example:**
Our single-region Kafka cluster — region outage = Kafka outage. Implemented MirrorMaker — primary region to secondary region replication. Region failover: tested, worked. Replication lag: 5 seconds — acceptable for DR. But MirrorMaker was single instance — SPOF. Made highly available — 3 MirrorMaker instances, consumer group. MirrorMaker failure: automatic failover. Also added monitoring — replication lag alert at 30 seconds. Caught network issue — lag spiked to 5 minutes, alerted, fixed before failover needed.

**Rule of thumb:** MirrorMaker for DR — cross-region replication. HA MirrorMaker — multiple instances, consumer group. Monitor replication lag — alert before lag exceeds RPO. Test failover — regular DR drills.
