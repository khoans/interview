## 272. What is the difference between Adapter and Facade?

**Adapter makes incompatible interfaces work together — translates one interface to another. Facade simplifies complex subsystem — provides easier interface.**

Adapter changes interface. Facade hides complexity.

**Adapter:**
- Problem: interface mismatch — client expects A, has B
- Solution: translate A calls to B
- Example: PayPal API → our PaymentProcessor interface

**Facade:**
- Problem: complexity — 10 methods to accomplish one task
- Solution: single method orchestrates 10 calls
- Example: VideoProcessingFacade.process() calls 8 subsystem methods

**Trade-off:**
- Adapter: integration without rewriting — but adds translation layer
- Facade: simplicity — but facade becomes bottleneck for advanced usage

**Real-world example:**
We integrated with legacy CRM — their SOAP API (200 methods, XML) didn't match our REST expectations (JSON, 10 methods). Adapter translated: our JSON → their XML, our method names → theirs. Integration: 1 week vs. 6 months CRM rewrite. Later, CRM usage grew complex — 5 adapter calls per business operation. Added Facade over Adapter: CustomerFacade.createCustomer() orchestrates 5 adapter calls. Callers: 5 lines → 1 line. Two layers: Facade (simplification) + Adapter (translation).

**Rule of thumb:** Adapter for interface mismatch. Facade for complexity. Can use both — Facade orchestrates, Adapter translates.
