## 394. What is account lockout policy?

**Account lockout policy temporarily locks account after multiple failed login attempts — prevents brute force attacks.**

It's about rate limiting authentication — too many failures = temporary lock.

**How it works:**
- Track failed attempts per account — 5 failures
- Lock account — prevent further attempts for 15 minutes
- Notify user — "Account locked, try again in 15 minutes"
- Auto-unlock — after timeout, reset failure count

**Trade-off:**
- Prevents brute force — attacker can't try unlimited passwords
- But: denial of service — attacker can lock legitimate users
- Balance: lockout threshold, duration must balance security and usability

**Real-world example:**
Our app had no lockout — attacker tried 10,000 passwords per account. 100 accounts compromised. Implemented lockout: 5 failures = 15-minute lock. Brute force: eliminated. But attacker exploited — targeted admin accounts, locked them repeatedly. Admins couldn't login (denial of service). Added IP-based rate limiting — 10 failures per IP = 1-hour IP ban. Attacker: can't lock accounts without changing IP. Admin availability: restored. Also, added email notification on lockout — users aware of attack.

**Rule of thumb:** Account lockout: 5-10 failures, 15-30 minute lock. IP rate limiting — prevent lockout abuse. Email notification — users aware of attacks. Admin accounts: additional protection (2FA, IP whitelist).
