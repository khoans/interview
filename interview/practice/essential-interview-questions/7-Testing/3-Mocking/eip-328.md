## 328. What is argument captor? How do you test with different arguments?

**Argument captor captures arguments passed to mock — verify complex objects, not just method was called.**

It's about testing what was passed — not just that method was called, but with what data.

**When to use:**
- Complex objects — can't easily compare all fields
- Modified objects — object created in method, passed to mock
- Multiple calls — verify different arguments for each call

**Trade-off:**
- Deep verification — check all fields of passed object
- But: verbose — more code than simple verify
- Overuse — captor for simple arguments, test becomes complex

**Real-world example:**
Our service created Email object internally — to, from, subject, body, attachments. verify(emailService).send(email) — but what email? ArgumentCaptor<Email> captured email. Verified: to == expected, subject == expected, body contains expected content. Caught bug: email sent to wrong address (typo in address construction). But captor added 10 lines per test. For simple arguments (String, int), used verify(mock).method(eq(expected)).

**Rule of thumb:** Argument captor for complex objects — verify all important fields. Simple arguments: verify(mock).method(expected). Don't captor primitives — use eq() matcher.
