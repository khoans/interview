## 327. What is verify() method?

**verify() checks if mock method was called — validates behavior, not just outcome.**

It's about testing interactions — did the code call the right methods with right arguments?

**Usage:**
- verify(mock).method() — method called once
- verify(mock, times(3)).method() — called 3 times
- verify(mock, never()).method() — never called
- verify(mock, atLeastOnce()).method() — called at least once

**Trade-off:**
- Validates behavior — ensures correct interactions
- But: fragile — refactor code, verify breaks even if behavior correct
- Over-verification — verify every call, tests become implementation details

**Real-world example:**
Our test verified 10 interactions — verify(emailService).send(), verify(logService).log(), verify(metrics).record()... Refactored: combined log and metrics. 5 tests broke — behavior correct, interactions changed. Switched to assert for outcomes — assert email sent (check inbox), assert metrics recorded (check database). Tests stable after refactoring. Kept verify for critical interactions — verify(paymentService).charge() — must be called exactly once.

**Rule of thumb:** Verify critical interactions — external calls, side effects. Assert outcomes — state changes, return values. Don't verify internal interactions — implementation details.
