## 309. What is acceptance testing?

**Acceptance testing validates software meets business requirements — customer/stakeholder approves before release.**

It's the final gate — does the software do what the business paid for?

**Types:**
- **User Acceptance Testing (UAT)** — end users test in production-like environment
- **Business Acceptance Testing** — business stakeholders verify requirements
- **Alpha/Beta testing** — limited release before full launch

**Trade-off:**
- Business validation — ensures software delivers value
- But: manual, time-consuming — days to weeks
- Late feedback — bugs found late are expensive to fix

**Real-world example:**
Our enterprise CRM had 3-month development cycle. UAT: 2 weeks, business users tested. Found critical bug: commission calculation wrong for multi-currency deals. $10M in deals affected. Fixed: 2 weeks, release delayed. But UAT caught bug before production — avoided $500K in incorrect commissions. Later moved to agile — acceptance tests automated, business stakeholders review every sprint. Bugs caught in sprint, not after 3 months.

**Rule of thumb:** Acceptance testing for business requirement validation. Automate acceptance criteria — executable specifications. Don't wait until end — continuous acceptance testing each sprint.
