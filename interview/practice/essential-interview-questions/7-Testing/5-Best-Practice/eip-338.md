## 338. What is given-when-then pattern?

**Given-When-Then structures tests as narrative — Given (context), When (action), Then (outcome).**

It's about storytelling in tests — describes business scenario, not just code.

**Three sections:**
- **Given** — initial context, preconditions, setup
- **When** — action taken, event triggered
- **Then** — expected outcome, assertions

**Trade-off:**
- Business-readable — non-developers understand test intent
- Workflow-focused — natural for multi-step scenarios
- But: verbose — more words than AAA
- Overkill for simple tests — Given: user exists, When: login, Then: success

**Real-world example:**
Our e-commerce tests used AAA — confusing for business stakeholders reviewing tests. Switched to Given-When-Then: "Given customer has items in cart, When checkout initiated, Then order created and inventory reserved." Business stakeholders could review tests, suggest scenarios. But simple unit tests became verbose — Given: two numbers, When: added, Then: sum correct. Reverted simple tests to AAA, kept Given-When-Then for integration/E2E tests.

**Rule of thumb:** Given-When-Then for integration/E2E tests — business scenarios. AAA for unit tests — simple, code-focused. Test names should tell story — shouldCreateOrder_WhenCheckout_WithItemsInCart.
