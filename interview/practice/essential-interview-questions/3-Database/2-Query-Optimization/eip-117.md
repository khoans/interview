## 117. What is the difference between WHERE and HAVING?

**WHERE filters rows before aggregation. HAVING filters groups after aggregation.**

Use WHERE to reduce data before grouping. Use HAVING to filter aggregated results.

**Key differences:**
- **WHERE** — operates on individual rows, evaluated before GROUP BY
- **HAVING** — operates on groups, evaluated after GROUP BY
- **WHERE** — cannot use aggregate functions (SUM, COUNT, AVG)
- **HAVING** — designed for aggregate functions

**Why it matters:**
- WHERE reduces data early — faster aggregation, less memory
- HAVING filters results — you've already paid the aggregation cost
- Wrong choice causes syntax errors or incorrect results

**Trade-off:**
- WHERE is more efficient — filters before expensive GROUP BY
- HAVING is necessary — no other way to filter on aggregates
- Combining both gives best performance — filter early, then filter groups

**Real-world example:**
Our sales report: `SELECT region, SUM(revenue) FROM orders GROUP BY region HAVING revenue > 10000`. Query took 12 seconds — aggregated all 10 million rows, then filtered to 50 regions. Optimized: added `WHERE order_date >= '2024-01-01'` to filter first, then `HAVING SUM(revenue) > 10000`. Query dropped to 800ms (15x faster). The WHERE clause reduced data from 10M to 500K rows before aggregation. HAVING then filtered 50 regions to 12 high-performing ones.

**Rule of thumb:** Filter as much as possible with WHERE before GROUP BY. Use HAVING only for aggregate conditions. Never put non-aggregate conditions in HAVING — move them to WHERE.
