## 111. What is index fragmentation? How do you fix it?

**Index fragmentation occurs when index pages are scattered across disk or have empty space — caused by frequent INSERTs, UPDATEs, and DELETEs.**

It degrades read performance because the database must seek across non-contiguous disk locations.

**Types of fragmentation:**
- **External fragmentation** — index pages not physically adjacent on disk (random I/O)
- **Internal fragmentation** — pages have empty space, index is larger than necessary

**Causes:**
- Random inserts (UUIDs as primary keys)
- Page splits from inserting into middle of sorted data
- Deletes leaving empty space in pages
- Updates that change indexed column values

**How to fix:**
- **Rebuild index** — drops and recreates, eliminates all fragmentation (expensive, locks table)
- **Reorganize index** — defragments in place, less effective but online (no locks)
- **Fill factor tuning** — leave space on pages for future growth (reduces page splits)

**Trade-off:**
- Rebuild is fast but locks the table — downtime for large tables
- Reorganize is online but slower and less thorough
- Fill factor reduces fragmentation but increases initial index size

**Real-world example:**
Our orders table used UUIDs as primary keys — random inserts caused 85% fragmentation in 6 months. Index size: 40GB (should be 15GB). Query performance degraded 3x over 6 months. Weekly rebuilds caused 2-hour maintenance windows. Switched to sequential IDs (Snowflake algorithm), fragmentation dropped to 5%. Eliminated rebuilds, maintenance window gone. For existing fragmentation, we used online reorganize during off-peak hours — zero downtime.

**Rule of thumb:** Monitor fragmentation monthly. Reorganize at 10-30%, rebuild above 30%. Use sequential keys to prevent fragmentation in the first place.
