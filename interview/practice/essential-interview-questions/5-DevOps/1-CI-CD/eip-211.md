## 211. What is canary deployment?

**Canary deployment rolls out new version to a small percentage of users first — 5%, then 25%, then 100% — monitoring for errors before full rollout.**

It's risk mitigation — if the new version has bugs, only 5% of users are affected, not 100%.

**How it works:**
- Deploy new version alongside old version
- Route 5% of traffic to new version (canary)
- Monitor metrics — error rates, latency, business KPIs
- If healthy, increase to 25%, then 50%, then 100%
- If errors spike, rollback canary (only 5% affected)

**Trade-off:**
- Risk reduction — bugs affect small % of users, not everyone
- Real-world testing — production traffic, real user behavior
- But: complex routing — need smart load balancing, session affinity
- Database compatibility — both versions must work with same schema

**Real-world example:**
Our payment service deployed with blue-green — full traffic switch. Bug in tax calculation: 100% of users affected, 1000 incorrect charges, 4-hour rollback. Switched to canary: 5% → 25% → 100% over 2 hours. Next deployment: bug in currency conversion, detected at 5% stage (10 errors). Rolled back canary, fixed, redeployed. 5% of users saw error for 10 minutes vs. 100% for 4 hours. Customer complaints: 1000 → 5.

**Rule of thumb:** Canary for high-risk deployments (payments, checkout, authentication). Automate rollout — increase % every 15 minutes if metrics healthy. Automated rollback on error spike (>1% error rate).
